Regina Briseño

Economist · Researcher

I am an economist with research interests in inflation dynamics and applied microeconometrics. My work focuses on price-setting behavior, cost pass-through, and the role of sectoral linkages in shaping inflation outcomes.

I hold a B.A. in Economics from ITAM. I have worked as an economist at the Directorate General of Economic Research at Banco de México. I am preparing to apply to PhD programs in Economics.

Research

Working Paper

Inflation Volatility across Advanced and Emerging Economies during the COVID-19 Pandemic
with L. Arango-Castillo and M. J. Orraca
Banco de México Working Paper No. 2025-13

This paper studies the relationship between inflation levels and inflation volatility during the COVID-19 period using data from over sixty economies. Using GARCH-based specifications, we show that inflation volatility is strongly associated with commodity price shocks and periods of heightened global uncertainty.

Works in Progress

Industry-Specific Cost Indices to Monitor Pressures on Inflation
with A. Espinoza, V. López, and M. J. Orraca

This project constructs industry-level cost indices using input–output tables to disentangle the sources of inflationary pressures in Mexico. The indices are designed to separate cost-driven pressures from demand-driven dynamics and to improve monitoring of inflation risks.

Producer Price Index Inflation Variance Transmission within Manufacturing Sectors
with L. Arango-Castillo and M. J. Orraca

This project studies the transmission of PPI inflation variance across manufacturing sectors in Mexico, the United States, and Canada, focusing on cross-sectoral and cross-country spillovers arising from input–output linkages and regional production networks.

Undergraduate Thesis

Asymmetric Pass-Through and Competition in Isolated Mexican Gasoline Markets
Undergraduate thesis, ITAM

This thesis examines retail gasoline pricing in Mexico following market deregulation. I construct a novel measure of local competition in geographically isolated markets and document asymmetric cost pass-through, with stronger price responses to cost increases than to decreases.
