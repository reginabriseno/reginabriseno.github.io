Working Paper

Inflation Volatility across Advanced and Emerging Economies during the COVID-19 Pandemic

with L. Arango-Castillo and M. J. Orraca

Banco de México Working Paper No. 2025-13

This paper studies the relationship between inflation levels and inflation volatility during the COVID-19 period using data from over sixty economies. Using GARCH-based specifications, we show that inflation volatility is strongly associated with commodity price shocks and periods of heightened global uncertainty.

Work in Progress

Industry-specific cost indices to monitor pressures on inflation

with A. Espinoza, V. López, and M. J. Orraca

Measuring cost pressures is important to inform policy decisions. We propose a method to construct industry-specific cost indices using I-O tables and estimate these for the most disaggregated level of the industrial classification in Mexico. By linking cost indices at the industry level with each item in the CPI basket in Mexico,we show that these are a powerful tool to illustrate how cost pressures have evolved over time, to analyze the sources of cost pressures on consumer prices in Mexico at different points in time, and particularly for understanding the inflationary surge in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. Finally, we show that cost indices have predictive power over inflation, despite pass-through being incomplete and lagged. This illustrates their usefulness to monitor the risks to inflation.

Work in Progress

Producer Price Index inflation variance transmission within the manufacturing sector of Mexico, the US and Canada

with L. Arango-Castillo and M. J. Orraca

We study the degree to which industries at different stages of the production process in Mexico, the United States, and Canada transmit inflation variance to industries in other stages and countries within the region. We use the Diebold-Yilmaz framework to calculate the connectedness of annual producer price inflation in the manufacturing industries from 2005 to 2024. We find that during the period of analysis, on average, 69.3\% of the inflation variance within these industries may be explained by shocks to industries in other stages or countries within the region. Using aggregate directional connectedness measures and descriptive evidence of trade flows, we argue that this can be rationalized by integrated production networks and large trade flows within the region. The results highlight that economic integration may expose countries to the variability of inflation in other countries.

Undergraduate Thesis

Asymmetric Pass-Through and Competition in Isolated Mexican Gasoline Markets

ITAM

This thesis examines retail gasoline pricing in Mexico following market deregulation. I construct a novel measure of local competition in geographically isolated markets and document asymmetric cost pass-through, with stronger price responses to cost increases than to decreases.
